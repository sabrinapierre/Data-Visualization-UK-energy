The UK proves cutting carbon dioxide emissions and increasing usage of renewable energy does not endanger economic growth.

Considering the five largest economies in the world, the United Kingdom has visibly managed to not only maintain lower carbon dioxide emissions, but decrease them over time.

The United Kingdom has only further grown its economy after adopting its 2008 Climate Change Act where they commit to decreasing greenhouse gas emissions to net zero by 2050, compared to 1990 levels.

The UK climate policies have altogether led to a decrease in fossil fuel (e.g. coal, gas, oil, nuclear) usage and a shift towards renewable energy sources (e.g. wind, solar, hydropower).

The UK's green policies have managed to decrease their fossil fuel usage without sacrificing their GDP per capita growth. Altogether the average carbon footprint of a United Kingdom resident has decreased while their wealth has increased.
